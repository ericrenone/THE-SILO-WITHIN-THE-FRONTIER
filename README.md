## 1. Two Definitions of Multi-Domain

The word *multi-domain* admits two distinct readings that are almost never separated in public discourse about researchers, and the conflation of these two readings is precisely the error this document corrects.

**The first reading is intra-paradigm breadth**: the ability to work across the recognized subdomains of a single epistemological framework. A deep learning researcher who works on computer vision, natural language processing, and reinforcement learning has intra-paradigm breadth. They command the full toolkit of the framework — gradient descent, attention mechanisms, normalization techniques, scaling laws — and can deploy it across different empirical domains. This researcher is legitimately broader than a specialist confined to a single subdomain. But they remain within the paradigm's variable space: every tool they import from one subfield to another is an epistemological cousin of every other tool in the family. The journals are adjacent. The conferences overlap. The hiring committees know how to evaluate the work. The citation network is connected.

**The second reading is inter-paradigm synthesis**: the importation of tools from a genuinely foreign epistemological framework — one with different journals, different training programs, different canonical methods, different open problems — into the native field's problem space. The algebraic number theorist who applies tools from their field to combinatorial geometry is performing inter-paradigm synthesis. The economist who recognizes that a bargaining problem is actually a question in algebraic topology is performing inter-paradigm synthesis. The physician who recognizes that a diagnostic problem is actually a question in Bayesian network theory is performing inter-paradigm synthesis.

**The two readings are not on a continuum. They are categorically distinct.**

Kuhn's *The Structure of Scientific Revolutions* (1962) establishes the relevant unit: the paradigm is not merely a specialty but an epistemological framework — a set of canonical problems, methods, exemplary solutions, and legitimating institutions that together constitute a way of doing science. Work within the paradigm is *normal science*: the filling in of details, extension to new cases, refinement of methods. Work that crosses paradigm boundaries is *revolutionary science* — and it is, as Kuhn documents, systematically resisted by the paradigm's institutions.

The ERIE-SCHOOL framework's analysis of the Erdős unit distance problem (THE-SILOED-MIND, 2026) is a claim about the second reading applied to research. Litt's diagnosis is precise: the problem sat unsolved for decades not because the native paradigm (combinatorial geometry) lacked talented people, but because the solution required tools from algebraic number theory — a different paradigm with different journals, different training pipelines, different prestige hierarchies. The combinatorial geometers who had read everything in their field were maximally informed *within it*. The information they were missing was that algebraic number theory existed and was relevant — information that required inhabiting both fields at a level sufficient to recognize the structural isomorphism.

Karpathy and Sutskever are both, by ERIE metrics, in the enlarged silo: the deep learning paradigm, spanning many subdomains, is their epistemological home. What follows is the documentation.

### The Pedagogical Work as Paradigm Legibility, Not Paradigm Crossing

Karpathy's most publicly visible work is pedagogical: CS231n made deep learning accessible to hundreds of thousands of students; "Zero to Hero" made the implementation of LLMs explicit at every step. Nathan and Petrosino (2003) distinguish two things that look similar from outside but are structurally different: **expert blind spot** (the inability to simulate what novices find difficult) and **expert transparency** (the ability to make tacit knowledge explicit and accessible). Karpathy's pedagogical work is expert transparency at an exceptional level — he is the field's best explicator of its own tacit knowledge.

Expert transparency and inter-paradigm synthesis are not the same cognitive operation. Expert transparency requires inhabiting the paradigm deeply enough to decompose it. Inter-paradigm synthesis requires leaving the paradigm far enough to recognize its limits from outside. The "Zero to Hero" curriculum does the former with virtuosity. It does not do the latter at all: the curriculum is organized around making the paradigm's tools learnable, not around recognizing which problems require tools the paradigm does not have.

This is Polanyi's (1958) *Personal Knowledge* distinction in application: tacit knowledge is paradigm-internal. Making tacit knowledge explicit is the work of the expert transparency function. The inter-paradigm move requires crossing from one body of tacit knowledge to another — which cannot be done by making the first body more explicit, only by inhabiting the second.

### Vibe Coding: The Closest Approach

Karpathy's February 2025 coinage of "vibe coding" — describing how AI tools allow non-programmers to construct applications by typing prompts — is his closest approach to genuine inter-paradigm synthesis in his public record. It imports a concept from improvisation and social epistemology (the "vibe" — tacit, intuitive, non-formalizable knowledge) into software development methodology. It represents a recognition that the practice of software development, as reshaped by LLMs, requires a framework that the paradigm of formal software engineering does not provide.

By ERIE standards: this is the gesture of inter-paradigm synthesis — a reaching toward a foreign framework's conceptual tools. It produced a widely adopted term. It did not produce a research program that formally develops the connection, imports tools from improvisation theory or embodied cognition into software methodology, and solves problems within software engineering that formal methods could not.

The observation is abductive. The synthesis is not complete. The ERIE outlier's abductive move is the beginning of a research program, not only a terminological contribution.

### The Scaling Hypothesis as Intra-Paradigm Abduction

Sutskever's most consequential contribution to AI strategy was the early recognition that scaling compute, data, and parameters would produce qualitative capability improvements. This is an abductive claim in Peirce's sense — a "best available explanation" from surprising observations — and it was correct when most of the paradigm's practitioners were skeptical.

By ERIE standards: this is intra-paradigm abduction at its best. The abduction is within the paradigm's variable space — it makes a claim about what the paradigm's own methods will produce when extended along its own dimensions. The ERIE outlier's abductive move is categorically different: it makes a claim that *a different paradigm's tools are necessary for a problem the native paradigm cannot solve*. Sutskever's scaling hypothesis claims the opposite: that the native paradigm's tools are sufficient — that they just need more of the same.

History has partially vindicated this. But it has also revealed the limits: the problems that remain unsolved (generalization theory, formal alignment, consciousness) are precisely the ones where "more of the same" has not produced the breakthrough. Those are the problems where the ERIE outlier's contribution would concentrate.

### The "Slightly Conscious" Observation as an Unrealized Gesture

This was the most significant public reaching-toward-inter-paradigm-synthesis in his record. It recognized that the behavior of large language models might require a framework — philosophy of mind, consciousness science — that the native ML paradigm does not provide tools for.

The ERIE framework's evaluation of this observation is precise: the abductive recognition is there. The paradigm crossing is not. As of May 2026 — four years after the tweet — there is no published research program from Sutskever that imports the tools of philosophy of consciousness (Integrated Information Theory, Global Workspace Theory, Higher-Order Theories, the hard problem literature) into neural architecture design or training methodology. The observation generated philosophical debate. It did not generate the inter-paradigm synthesis that would make it scientifically productive.

By ERIE standards: necessary but not sufficient. The reaching is visible. The tools-level importation did not follow.

## 4. The Prestige Copying Chain as the Mechanism of Production

Both figures are, by ERIE-SCHOOL standards, products of the prestige copying chain that THE-SILOED-MIND document analyzes. Tracing the chain makes this structurally precise.

### Node 1: Doctoral Advisors

- **Karpathy**: Fei-Fei Li, Stanford — the dominant figure in computer vision within the neural network paradigm; the architect of ImageNet; within-paradigm depth at maximum.
- **Sutskever**: Geoffrey Hinton, Toronto — the originating figure of the deep learning paradigm itself; the source of backpropagation as practice; the center of the paradigm's genealogy.

Both advisors are figures of maximum paradigm depth. Both trained their students to win the paradigm's internal signaling competitions. Neither advisor's primary identity is as a paradigm-boundary crosser. Hinton's work, for all its brilliance, is not algebraic topology applied to learning theory — it is the extension and deepening of the neural network framework Rosenblatt initiated. The training environment selected for paradigm depth, not paradigm crossing.

### Node 2: Publication Venues

Both were trained to publish in the paradigm's recognized venues: NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, CVPR. These venues are the field-specific journal structure that THE-SILOED-MIND identifies as the silo's enforcement mechanism. Review committees are composed of paradigm insiders. Cross-paradigm work is systematically not their scope. A paper that applied algebraic topology to understand the learning dynamics of transformers would face review committees that could evaluate either the topology or the transformers but not both simultaneously.

### Node 3: Hiring Context

Both were hired by OpenAI — the paradigm's most resource-intensive node — on the basis of within-paradigm performance signals. OpenAI selected for paradigm depth and paradigm-internal vision. Karpathy brought the paradigm's best pedagogical translator; Sutskever brought the paradigm's most successful research strategist. Neither was hired for inter-paradigm synthesis capacity. Neither was evaluated for it.

### Node 4: Career Trajectory

- **Karpathy**: Stanford → OpenAI → Tesla → OpenAI → Anthropic pretraining. Each step is deeper into the paradigm's core infrastructure. The move to Anthropic pretraining is the deepest node of any he has occupied.
- **Sutskever**: Hinton's lab → Google Brain → OpenAI chief scientist → SSI. Each step is further toward the paradigm's most consequential applications. SSI is the most potentially boundary-crossing move — but the research direction has not yet produced the formal, tools-level inter-paradigm synthesis that the ERIE framework predicts is necessary for genuine technical alignment progress.

The prestige copying chain selected for both and rewarded both at every node. The chain is not selecting against them. It is selecting precisely *for* them — for the profile it knows how to evaluate. What it is not selecting for, and what neither figure's career has been shaped toward, is the inter-paradigm boundary crossing that the ERIE framework identifies as the concentrated point of breakthrough probability.

## 6. The ERIE Outlier Contrast: What Inter-Paradigm Synthesis Actually Looks Like

The Erdős case is the touchstone. What made the breakthrough possible was not greater depth within combinatorial geometry. It was the recognition that algebraic number theory — a field with different journals (*Journal of Number Theory*, *Inventiones Mathematicae*), different training programs (algebraic rather than combinatorial), different open problems (L-functions, elliptic curves, class field theory), different prestige hierarchies — was relevant to a problem the combinatorial geometry community had been attacking with its own tools for decades.

The ERIE outlier working on deep learning would be making moves like:

**Move 1: Algebraic Geometry → Generalization Theory**
The neural network generalization problem — why do overparameterized networks generalize, when classical statistical learning theory (VC dimension) predicts they should not? — is currently unsolved. Recent work by researchers with genuine contributory expertise in both algebraic geometry and machine learning (e.g., the persistent homology approach of Carlsson and colleagues; the tensor decomposition approach drawing on algebraic geometry) represents the beginning of inter-paradigm synthesis on this problem. These researchers are not the most cited figures at NeurIPS. They are at the paradigm boundary.

**Move 2: Formal Verification → Technical Alignment**
The alignment problem at the formal level — how to verify that a trained neural network satisfies a specified behavioral constraint across its entire input distribution — is a question in model checking and formal verification. Φ_formal-methods has its own journals (*Formal Aspects of Computing*, *ACM TOPLAS*), its own conferences (CAV, LICS), its own training programs, and its own canonical problems (temporal logic model checking, abstract interpretation). The inter-paradigm synthesis requires people with contributory expertise in both fields. Neither Karpathy nor Sutskever's primary contributions import from Φ_formal-methods at the tools level.

**Move 3: Philosophy of Mind → Interpretability**
The interpretability problem — what is the model doing internally, at the level of representations — reaches quickly toward questions that philosophy of mind has worked on for decades: propositional vs. sub-propositional representations, the binding problem, the distinction between syntactic and semantic content. Friston's free energy principle, Tononi's integrated information theory, Dehaene's global workspace theory — these are paradigms with tools (mathematical formalism, experimental paradigms, theoretical frameworks) that the interpretability community has not imported at the tools level. The researchers who will solve interpretability may need to be working in both Φ_DL and Φ_philosophy-of-mind.

**Move 4: Statistical Physics → Training Dynamics**
The double descent phenomenon, loss landscape geometry, and emergent capabilities as phase transitions are all problems that statistical physics (Φ_stat-phys) has tools for: renormalization group theory, mean field theory, phase transition theory, spin glasses. Some researchers (e.g., Bahri, Sohl-Dickstein and colleagues on neural tangent kernels; Goldt and colleagues on teacher-student networks) have begun making this importation. They are making the ERIE outlier's move. They are also not the most cited figures at NeurIPS.

All four represent the Litt moment: problems that have been worked on by paradigm insiders for years, whose solution requires tools from a paradigm the insiders have not invested in. The ERIE outlier's comparative advantage concentrates precisely here. Neither Karpathy nor Sutskever's primary public record shows the move at this level.

### Boden's Transformational Creativity at the Inter-Paradigm Level

Boden (2004) distinguishes three creativity types:
1. **Combinatorial**: producing novel combinations of familiar ideas within a conceptual space
2. **Exploratory**: exploring the limits of a defined conceptual space
3. **Transformational**: changing the rules of the conceptual space itself

Karpathy's primary creative operation is exploratory and combinatorial within Φ_DL: exploring what the neural network paradigm makes possible across different empirical domains. His pedagogy is explicitly exploratory — mapping the full extent of the paradigm's conceptual space.

Sutskever's primary creative operation is transformational *within* the paradigm's ontology: he has changed what the paradigm treats as its central task (from narrow performance to large-scale language modeling, from supervised learning to RLHF) without changing the paradigm's fundamental epistemological tools.

The ERIE outlier performs transformational creativity **at the level of the paradigm's fundamental ontology**: recognizing that a different ontology (algebraic, topological, logical, phenomenological) is required to solve problems the current paradigm frames but cannot solve. This is Boden's transformational creativity applied not to a problem space but to the framework that generates the problem space.

## 7. The Silo at the Paradigm Level: Formal Statement

Let Φ_i denote an epistemological framework (paradigm in Kuhn's sense): the set of canonical problems, methods, exemplary solutions, and legitimating institutions that together constitute a way of doing science.

Let Ψ(R, Φ) denote researcher R's contributory expertise (Collins & Evans, 2002) within paradigm Φ: not interactional expertise (knowing the concepts) but contributory expertise (able to produce results recognizable within the paradigm as contributions).

Let B(R, Φ_i → Φ_j) denote the inter-paradigm boundary crossing capacity of R: the demonstrated ability to recognize that a problem in Φ_i requires tools from Φ_j ≠ Φ_i and to import those tools at the level of contributory expertise.

**The Intra-Paradigm Generalist:**

R is an intra-paradigm generalist if:
```
Ψ(R, Φ_DL) is high across multiple subdomains of Φ_DL
B(R, Φ_DL → Φ_j) ≈ 0 for all Φ_j ∉ {Φ_DL, Φ_DL-adjacent}
Institutional_legibility(R, Φ_DL) is high
```

**Karpathy and Sutskever:**

Both satisfy:
```
Ψ(R, Φ_DL) = very high across {CV, NLP, RL, scaling, safety} ⊂ Φ_DL
B(R, Φ_DL → Φ_algebraic-geometry) ≈ 0  (no major contributions)
B(R, Φ_DL → Φ_formal-verification) ≈ 0  (Sutskever reaches toward; no tools-level importation)
B(R, Φ_DL → Φ_philosophy-of-mind) ≈ 0  (Sutskever gestures; no research program follows)
B(R, Φ_DL → Φ_stat-phys) ≈ 0  (scaling hypothesis is empirical, not stat-phys formal)
Institutional_legibility(R, Φ_DL) = maximum (TIME100, FRS, NAS, NeurIPS ×3)
```

**The ERIE Outlier:**

```
Ψ(O, Φ_i) ≥ threshold for multiple genuinely distinct Φ_j ≠ Φ_i
B(O, Φ_i → Φ_j) > 0 — evidenced in primary contributions
Institutional_legibility(O, Φ_i) < maximum for each Φ individually
  [the work is partially illegible to each paradigm's apparatus alone]
```

**The Relative Silo Measure:**

Define:
```
Silo(R) = 1 − [Σ_j B(R, Φ_DL → Φ_j) × competence_weight(Φ_j)] / N_relevant_paradigms
```

where N_relevant_paradigms is the number of paradigms whose tools are relevant to the current breakthrough frontier.

For Karpathy and Sutskever:
```
Silo(Karpathy) → 1  [by this metric; B ≈ 0 for all relevant Φ_j]
Silo(Sutskever) → 1  [same; gestures toward Φ_j without tools-level B]
```

For the ERIE outlier:
```
Silo(O) < Silo(Karpathy) and Silo(Sutskever)
[even if O's Ψ within Φ_DL is lower]
```

The specific irony: the prestige apparatus measures Ψ(R, Φ_DL). The breakthrough frontier requires B(R, Φ_DL → Φ_j). The apparatus's signal and the frontier's requirement are orthogonal. Maximum apparatus recognition does not correlate with maximum frontier capacity. For Karpathy and Sutskever, it may be inversely correlated: the investment in paradigm depth that produced their apparatus recognition is the same investment that foreclosed the paradigm-crossing.

## 8. What Neither Will Build: Three Foreclosed Syntheses

The most consequential finding of this analysis is not about Karpathy or Sutskever individually. It is about what the deep learning paradigm cannot see from within itself — and about what the institutional apparatus selecting for paradigm depth is producing in the researchers it trains next.

### Foreclosed Synthesis 1: The Generalization Proof

Why do neural networks with more parameters than training examples generalize rather than memorize? Classical statistical learning theory (Vapnik-Chervonenkis theory, Rademacher complexity) predicts they should not. The double descent phenomenon — where test error improves beyond the classical bias-variance tradeoff as overparameterization increases — remains theoretically unexplained at the level of proof.

The tools required are from at least three paradigms: statistical learning theory (Φ_SLT), algebraic geometry (Φ_AG, for the loss landscape geometry), and statistical physics (Φ_SP, for phase transitions in complex systems). The inter-paradigm synthesis requires researchers with contributory expertise in all three. The prestige copying chain in Φ_DL does not produce this. It produces people who can train models. The people who can prove the generalization theorem are at the intersection of these paradigms, are not the most cited figures at NeurIPS, and are not the people that OpenAI, Anthropic, or Google DeepMind are competing most fiercely to hire.

Neither Karpathy nor Sutskever's training, career trajectory, or public contribution record positions them to make this synthesis.

### Foreclosed Synthesis 2: Formal Technical Alignment

The alignment problem at the strategic level — Sutskever's domain — asks: what should AI development prioritize? The alignment problem at the technical level asks: how do we formally verify that a trained neural network satisfies a specified behavioral constraint across its entire input distribution?

The technical alignment problem is a question in formal verification and model checking (Φ_FM): the branch of computer science concerned with proving that systems satisfy specifications. Clarke, Emerson, and Sifakis received the Turing Award in 2007 for model checking. Φ_FM has its own journals, its own conferences (CAV, LICS, ATVA), its own training pipelines, and its own open problems. Importing Φ_FM's tools into neural network verification requires people with contributory expertise in both Φ_DL and Φ_FM.

Sutskever's SSI is attempting the problem at the level of organizational strategy and research direction. The technical version requires the inter-paradigm synthesis. The synthesis has not yet been made at the level that would produce a formal verification framework for large neural networks — and neither Karpathy's nor Sutskever's career trajectory has positioned them to make it.

### Foreclosed Synthesis 3: The Consciousness / Interpretability Convergence

Sutskever's 2022 "slightly conscious" observation points toward a real problem: what the internal representations of large language models are, whether they constitute anything like intentionality, and how to evaluate claims about their internal states. Philosophy of mind has worked on the corresponding questions for decades: propositional attitudes, the binding problem, the distinction between syntactic and semantic content, the hard problem of consciousness.

The interpretability research program at Anthropic (where Karpathy now works) and elsewhere is approaching this empirically: mapping circuits, identifying features, tracing information flow. But the fundamental question — whether the representations constitute something that deserves the name of meaning, and if so what kind — is a philosophy of mind question. Answering it requires importing tools from Φ_philosophy-of-mind (the conceptual framework of Intentionality, of quale, of the explanatory gap) into the interpretability research program.

Neither Karpathy nor Sutskever has published work that makes this import at the tools level. Sutskever's tweet gestured toward it in 2022. As of May 2026, four years later, the tools-level importation has not followed in his public record.

All three represent the Litt moment: a problem that paradigm insiders have been working on for years, that requires tools the paradigm does not have. The ERIE outlier's breakthrough probability concentrates precisely here. The prestige apparatus is not currently finding the researchers positioned to make these syntheses.

## 9. The Paradigm-Level Institutional Delegation Cascade

THE-COGNITIVE-TAX document (ERI Labs, 2026) analyzes the Institutional Delegation Cascade: the mechanism by which a school's failure to identify a child's capacity sends a null signal back through the family, which reads the null signal as a performance verdict and deepens domestic suppression with institutional authority behind it. The same mechanism operates at the paradigm level.

**The Cascade at Paradigm Scale:**

1. **Delegation**: Funding agencies, universities, and AI labs delegate the identification of the most consequential researchers to the paradigm's prestige apparatus: citation counts, publication venue, NeurIPS oral acceptances, lab affiliation.

2. **Identification failure**: The apparatus is not calibrated for inter-paradigm synthesis capacity. A researcher working at the intersection of algebraic geometry and machine learning produces signals that each paradigm's apparatus evaluates as inadequate: not algebraic geometry enough for *Inventiones Mathematicae*; not applied enough for NeurIPS. The apparatus returns a null or mixed signal.

3. **Signal return**: The null signal reaches the funding agencies, the hiring committees, the senior researchers who advise early-career trajectories.

4. **Interpretation failure**: The null signal is read as a performance verdict. "If this work were consequential, the paradigm's apparatus would have found it." The apparatus is assumed to be reliable. It is not calibrated for what it is claiming to evaluate.

5. **Cascade completion**: Investment deepens toward Karpathy-type and Sutskever-type profiles — paradigm-internal generalists with maximum apparatus legibility. The inter-paradigm researcher does not receive the support that would have developed the synthesis.

6. **Reinforcement**: Each Karpathy and Sutskever success within the paradigm appears to confirm the apparatus's reliability. The cascade is self-referential: the apparatus selects for the type of work it can evaluate, that work succeeds within the paradigm, and the success is taken as evidence that the apparatus is correct.

Sunstein and Thaler's (2008) informational cascade analysis applies precisely: each node in the chain provides apparent evidence that others have independently evaluated the underlying value of paradigm depth. The journal exists because hiring committees value publications in it. The hiring committee values publications in it because funding panels support the work that produces them. No node is asking: does this research agenda cross the field boundaries that the most important current problems require crossing?

## 11. Falsifiable Predictions

**P1 — Citation Network Topology of the Generalization Breakthrough**
The researchers who formally solve the neural network generalization problem — proving why overparameterized networks generalize — will have publication records that span at least two genuinely distinct citation networks: the standard ML venues (NeurIPS, ICML) and at least one foreign paradigm's venues (algebraic geometry journals, dynamical systems journals, statistical mechanics journals). These researchers will have lower NeurIPS citation counts than Karpathy or Sutskever at comparable career stages. The breakthrough will not come primarily from within the deep learning paradigm's highest-prestige hiring pool.

**P2 — The Sutskever Consciousness Gap**
Sutskever's "slightly conscious" observation (February 2022) will not produce a research program with results published in both AI venues and philosophy of mind venues within five years of the observation (by February 2027). The observation was made without the tools of the foreign paradigm; the ERIE framework predicts that the tools-level importation does not follow from the observation alone. Prediction testable against Sutskever's publication record through February 2027.

**P3 — Karpathy's Pedagogical Legacy and Paradigm Boundaries**
Karpathy's "Zero to Hero" and similar curriculum will produce excellent intra-paradigm practitioners who can implement and extend the paradigm's tools. It will not produce, at disproportionate rates, inter-paradigm synthesizers. This is predicted because the curriculum is organized around making the paradigm's tacit knowledge explicit — not around developing the recognition that the paradigm's tools are insufficient and that a different paradigm's tools are required. Testable by curriculum analysis and longitudinal tracking of graduates' contribution types.

**P4 — The Alignment Synthesis and Publication Venue**
The technical alignment progress that produces formal guarantees — not behavioral evaluation but formal verification — will come from researchers with publication records at the intersection of Φ_formal-methods and Φ_DL. These researchers will not be the current most-cited figures in either field. They will be at the paradigm boundary, under-selected by both paradigms' prestige apparatuses. Testable by tracking breakthrough attribution when formal verification for large neural networks is achieved.

**P5 — Paradigm-Legibility and Breakthrough Type**
Controlling for intellectual ability (as best measurable), the researchers most likely to produce inter-paradigm syntheses in AI will have lower institutional legibility within Φ_DL's prestige apparatus — lower NeurIPS citation counts, fewer affiliations with the highest-prestige labs — than the researchers who produce intra-paradigm advances. The prestige apparatus selects for what it can evaluate; inter-paradigm synthesis capacity is specifically what it cannot evaluate.

**P6 — The ERIE Outlier at the Paradigm Boundary Is Currently Invisible**
The researchers who will make the three foreclosed syntheses identified in Section 8 are currently present in the research ecosystem, at the paradigm boundaries, and are not the subjects of TIME100 lists, FRS fellowships, or the highest-resource lab offers. This is the structural prediction of the ERIE framework applied at paradigm scale: the most consequential capacity is conditionally expressed, institutionally under-identified, and concentrated precisely where the prestige apparatus is not looking.

## Conclusion: The Paradigm Is the Silo

Andrej Karpathy and Ilya Sutskever are, by every metric the AI field applies, extraordinary figures. Their contributions have defined the paradigm that now shapes every consequential decision about how intelligence is being developed and deployed. Karpathy made the paradigm legible to hundreds of thousands of practitioners. Sutskever made the paradigm see further than it had seen before. Both have been rewarded by the paradigm's institutions with the recognition those institutions grant to their best contributors.

By ERIE metrics, both are siloed.

Not siloed in the narrow sense — not specialists in a single technical subfield, not incurious, not unable to apply their tools broadly. Siloed at the level of the paradigm itself: the deep learning framework is the epistemological environment within which their work operates, and the boundaries of that framework are the boundaries they have not crossed in their primary contributions.

The ERIE outlier's defining operation — absent from both figures' public records at the level of major contributions — is the recognition that a problem requires tools the paradigm does not have and the willingness, capacity, and positioning to import those tools from a genuinely foreign epistemological territory. This is the operation that solved the Erdős unit distance problem. This is the operation that Kapoor et al. (2026) confirm is the specific AI failure mode — the one humans retain the premium on. This is the operation that neither the prestige apparatus that produced Karpathy and Sutskever, nor the hiring practices at OpenAI and Anthropic, is currently structured to identify or reward.

The irony is specific: both Karpathy and Sutskever now work at AI safety organizations — the contexts in which inter-paradigm synthesis is most urgently needed. Anthropic's interpretability team and Sutskever's SSI are both pointed at problems (consciousness, alignment, generalization) that the inter-paradigm synthesis could unlock. Whether either figure will make the crossing is an open question. What this document establishes is that neither has made it yet at the level of their primary research contributions, and that the institutional apparatus that produced them is not structured to support the crossing if they attempt it.

The paradigm is the silo. It is a large, rich, consequential silo that has generated extraordinary science. It is still bounded. The problems at its boundaries are unsolved. The people positioned to solve them are not the people the prestige apparatus is finding.

That gap is not Karpathy's failure. It is not Sutskever's failure. It is the failure of the institutional logic that can evaluate paradigm depth and cannot evaluate paradigm crossing — and that rewards the former so thoroughly that the latter is perpetually underfunded, underhired, and uncited until the moment it solves the problem the paradigm spent decades not solving.

The Litt discomfort is the right response. The field should share it.
